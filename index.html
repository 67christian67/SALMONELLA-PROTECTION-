Critical defense against Salmonella & E. coli — follow CDC protocols to stop preventable foodborne threats in their tracks.

What is Salmonella?

Salmonella bacteria remain a top cause of foodborne illness (CDC data through 2026). Gram-negative rods from animal intestines contaminate food via fecal matter — outbreaks still common in poultry, produce, supplements.

Symptoms (6–72 hours post-exposure):
Bloody diarrhea • High fever • Severe cramps • Vomiting
Seek care immediately for dehydration, persistent blood, or high-risk groups.

High-risk groups:

  • Children under 5
  • Adults 65+
  • Immunocompromised
  • Pregnant individuals

Salmonella Prevention – CDC Core 4

Wash hands rigorously; never rinse raw poultry (spreads via splash); use thermometer religiously.

CLEAN • SEPARATE • COOK • CHILL
  • CLEAN: 20+ sec soap handwash after raw meat/bathroom/animals. Sanitize surfaces — no raw chicken rinse.
  • SEPARATE: Color-code boards; raw meats on bottom fridge shelf.
  • COOK: Poultry/ground meat 165°F (74°C); eggs firm. Thermometer mandatory.
  • CHILL: Refrigerate <2 hrs; ≤40°F fridge. Safe thaw only.
  • Avoid unpasteurized; don't cook if symptomatic.

E. coli Prevention

Pathogenic E. coli (e.g., O157:H7) shares prevention with Salmonella but hits harder on ground beef (cook to 160°F), raw produce, unpasteurized dairy. CDC stresses no unsafe water, thorough handwashing.

Especially dangerous for kids (HUS risk): Bloody diarrhea, no urine — get medical help fast.
  • Handwash 20+ sec — after bathroom/diapers/animals, before food prep.
  • Core 4: Especially cook ground beef 160°F (no pink); separate raw/cooked.
  • Rinse produce well; skip raw sprouts if high-risk.
  • Pasteurized dairy/juices only.
  • Safe water — no untreated wells/recreational swallowing.
  • Wash hands after petting zoos/farm animals.

Final Warning

One lapse in protocol can cause serious illness — or worse.

Treat every raw animal product as contaminated. Vigilance saves lives.
